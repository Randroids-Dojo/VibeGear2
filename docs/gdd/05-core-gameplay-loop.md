# 5. Core gameplay loop

The intended loop is:

Garage briefing → choose car/setup → race → results → repairs → upgrades → next race/tour unlock

## Race preparation

Pre-race preparation includes:

- Car selection if entering a new championship or buying a new car.
- Tire choice based on forecast and track surface mix.
- Optional suspension and gearing bias from the current tuning sheet.
- Nitro loadout confirmation.
- Brief look at track card: length, corner density, weather, visibility note, surface grip.

## Car selection and garage flow

At the start of a championship, the player chooses one starter car. Additional cars unlock by tour progression and credits. The garage is not just cosmetic; it is where the player sees:

- Installed upgrades.
- Damage state.
- Current cash.
- Tour standings.
- Recommended weather fit.
- Ghost or leaderboard status for the upcoming track.

## Rewards

Race rewards are the main currency source. Bonuses layer on top for:

- Podium.
- Fastest lap.
- Clean race.
- Underdog finish.
- Tour completion.
- Sponsor objective.

## Repairs

Repairs happen between races, never through a simulation-style pit stop. This preserves flow and keeps damage strategic rather than fiddly. The player can choose:

- Full repair.
- Essential repair only.
- Risk it and save money.

## Upgrades

Upgrades should be small, frequent, and legible. The player should almost always be able to afford something, but not everything.

## Progression unlocks

Progression should unlock:

- New tours.
- New cars.
- New upgrade tiers.
- New weather variants.
- New soundtrack layers or region themes.
- New challenge modes and ghosts.
