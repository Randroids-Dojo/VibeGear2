# 8. World and progression design

VibeGear2 should preserve the “four races per destination block” idea but package it into an original world.

### Structure

Full v1.0 target: 8 tours × 4 tracks = 32 tracks  
Stretch target: 10 tours × 4 tracks = 40 tracks

This is intentionally smaller than Top Gear 2’s 64-race scope because it is more realistic for a solo/open-source project while still preserving the mini-championship rhythm.

### Original tours

| Tour | Theme | Weather profile | Difficulty role |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Velvet Coast | Resorts, marinas, cliff roads | Clear, dusk, light rain | Onboarding |
| Iron Borough | Industrial ports and ring roads | Overcast, fog | Teaches traffic pressure |
| Ember Steppe | Desert highways and canyon walls | Heat haze, dust storm | Teaches speed and lane discipline |
| Breakwater Isles | Causeways and sea bridges | Rain, crosswind visuals | Teaches wet grip |
| Glass Ridge | Alpine passes and tunnels | Snow, fog, dusk | Teaches braking and setup |
| Neon Meridian | Futuristic metro outskirts | Night rain, neon glare | Teaches visibility adaptation |
| Moss Frontier | Forest highways and villages | Heavy rain, mist | Technical mid-late game |
| Crown Circuit | International finale hubs | Mixed and seeded dynamic weather | Endgame mastery |

### Unlock structure

- Tours unlock sequentially on first completion.
- Bronze-level continuation can unlock the next tour on easy.
- Full podium progression unlocks bonus content and alternate cars.
- Challenge medals unlock cosmetics, soundtrack remixes, and extra championships.
