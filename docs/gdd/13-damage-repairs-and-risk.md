# 13. Damage, repairs, and risk

## Damage sources

- Wall impacts
- Heavy traffic impacts
- Off-road impacts with hard scenery
- Nitro overuse in damaged state
- Repeated poor landings on hill crests

## Damage visualization

Use a compact quadrant display plus one overall health bar:

- Nose
- Flanks
- Rear
- Engine state indicator

Avoid imitating Top Gear 2’s exact silhouette or placement.

## Mechanical effects

- Front damage: braking instability and worse head-on collision recovery
- Side damage: more rubbing penalty and lane-hold wobble
- Rear damage: traction loss under acceleration
- Engine damage: reduced top speed and weaker nitro

## Repair decisions

After each race the player can choose:

- Full service
- Quick patch
- Skip repairs

Quick patch repairs the most performance-critical systems first.

## Race-ending damage threshold

Catastrophic damage should be rare but possible in hard mode. Normal mode should usually degrade the car into “limp but finishable” rather than hard-ending the run.

## Balancing principle

Damage should create tension, not hopelessness. If players routinely restart because one collision ruins the whole tour, the system is overtuned.
