# 4. Player experience goals

VibeGear2 should feel like this, moment to moment:

- You launch into a race quickly.
- The road reads clearly at speed.
- Passing one car feels easy; passing the lead pack feels earned.
- A mistake hurts, but recovery is possible.
- The next shop choice is obvious but not trivial.
- A tour feels beatable if you improve either your driving or your setup.

Session targets

| Session type | Duration target |
| --- | --- |
| Single quick race | 3 to 5 minutes |
| One four-race tour | 15 to 25 minutes |
| One full play session | 20 to 45 minutes |
| Time trial / ghost chase | 2 to 15 minutes |

## Difficulty curve

The difficulty curve should ramp in four layers:

| Layer | What changes |
| --- | --- |
| Early tours | Wide roads, readable curves, mild weather, low-speed opponents |
| Mid tours | Mixed braking zones, mild visibility effects, more punishing off-road |
| Late tours | Complex linked corners, lane congestion, harsher weather, taller speed ceiling |
| Final tours | Long-form consistency, heavy AI pressure, endurance on repairs and nitro use |

## Arcade versus simulation balance

VibeGear2 is an arcade racer with mechanical consequences, not a simulation. The player should not manage clutch bite, tire temperatures, pit menus, or detailed suspension telemetry. The correct level of complexity is:

- Simple inputs
- Meaningful setup
- Readable handling
- Stylized damage
- High learnability
- Low dead time

## One-more-race hooks

The game should maintain compulsion using the following loop triggers:

- Clear four-race tour structure.
- Close upgrade thresholds.
- “Just enough money” pacing.
- Visible standings movement.
- Track unlock reveals.
- Personal best and ghost goals.
- Fast restart and rematch.
